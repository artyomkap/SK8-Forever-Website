Tricks in Skateboarding

Skateboarding contains a huge number of tricks of different types and in different places.
The main types of tricks are:

  • Tricks on flat ground
  • Tricks in the ramps
  • Grinds and slides
  • Grabs
  • Manuals
Ollie:

The very first trick that all beginners begin to learn, after they have learned to feel free with ordinary skateboarding, is Ollie. The ollie is a trick that was invented by American skateboarder Alan Gelfand in 1976, but it was an ollie on a ramp, not like skateboarders are used to seeing these days. The flat ground ollie was invented by Rodney Mullen in 1982. This trick is done by flicking the board's tail to the ground and bringing the front foot up the board parallel to the board.

Kickflip:

The next one of the most popular tricks is the kickflip. This is a trick in which the board rotates 360 degrees about the longitudinal axis. It was invented again by Rodney Mullen in 1983. To perform a kickflip, the skateboarder takes to the air and lifts their back foot off the board while sliding their front foot off the skateboard diagonally forward and toward the heel of the foot. This movement of the front foot, sometimes called a "flick", rotates the board, completely flipping it over. Before landing, the rider stops the rotation by returning their feet to the board as it approaches its original position.

Manuals:

Manual is a trick in skateboarding which is a balance on the back or front wheels. A variation of this trick on the front wheels is called a manual, to perform this trick, you need to transfer the balance of the body to the back of the board and balance on the rear wheels. Another more complex variation of the nose manual is performed in the same way, only the weight is transferred to the nose of the board. This trick takes a long time to practice, first a skater can ride one meter balancing, then two, and after a long time of practice, the whole 10 meters.
